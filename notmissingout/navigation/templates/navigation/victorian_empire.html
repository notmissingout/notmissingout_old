Little Learners Explore the Victorian Empire
The Victorians had the largest recorded empire. They had extremely racist views and believed they were bringing "civilization" to the world. At this session we explored some of the countries that made up the empire, and learnt what use the Victorians had for them.
A Navy Gunship and a Merchant Vessel
The Victorians held their empire in check with their vast Navy and made money by trading around the world. We played with our own gunships and merchant vessels.
Australia
Unlike the Victorians we took a little time to learn about an aspect of Australian Aboriginal culture, dot paintings. We also pretended to be convicts working the land.
Ceylon
We played a pairs memory game with pictures from colonial Ceylon (and some modern day tea pickers in Sri Lanka). Some of us coloured pictures of tea pickers.
East Africa
We played with safari animals in the colony of East Africa (mostly modern day Kenya) and hunted for elephants to take their ivory tusks.
Cape Colony
In the Cape Colony (mostly modern day South Africa) we mined for diamonds and gold.
Canada and Jamaica
We traded timber and furs at the Canadian trading post and then sailed our goods back to England. In Jamaica we tried cocobread.
India
We ate curry, dressed in Indian clothes, played with spice scented play dough and had the option to play 'stick the jewel on the crown'.
New Zealand
We made our own poi and spun it to Maori music.
