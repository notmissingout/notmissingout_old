Little Learners Explore Victorian Entertainment
There was relatively little prep needed for this session but Henry did enjoy making slimey ectoplasm.
Ectoplasm Sensory Play
Many Victorians attempted to contact the dead, both in organised seances and through the use of talking boards at home. Mediums would produce objects they told people were physical manifestations of spirits. The special supernatural material (proved to be mostly cheesecloth and starch) was called ectoplasm. We made up our own "ectoplasm" and added some crepe banadges for a bit of spooky sensory play.
Curiosity Show
From so called mermaids to bearded ladies, the Victorians loved an unusual spectacle. Our curiosity tent boasted a large number of bearded ladies and more than a few mermaids.
The Circus
Circuses were extremely popular with the Victorians. Our performers rather enjoyed exhibiting their circus skills too (including the adults).
The Music Hall
Play food was served whilst listening to popular Victorian Music Hall songs.
The Theatre
There were costumes and facepaint ready for a theatrical performance, although in the end we were so busy with the circus there wasn't any time for plays!
Decoupage
Arts and crafts were popular with Victorian ladies. Those who could afford such things would beautify everyday objects by gluing brightly coloured paper onto them. We used this technique to decorate trinket boxes.
Quilling
Another popular Victorian craft was quilling, the art of making pictures from rolled pieces of paper.
Magic and Table Top Games
The Victorians enjoyed to watch illusionists perform magic shows. Some of us had a go at performing our own magic. We also played some of the games popular with the Victorians such as backgammon, draughts and reversi.
Victorian Sports
We played football and croquet in the garden. Some of us also "hunted" for some of the animals the Victorians shot for sport.
Fantascopes and Colouring
We had the opportunity to investigate how a Fantascope worked and make our own. There was also the usual themed wordsearches, colouring, and books to explore.
