Little Learners Explore Victorian Childhood
When preparing for this session Henry and I enjoyed reading lots of books about Victorian Childhood. We learnt that at the start of the Victorian era poor children worked very long hours, often in dangerous jobs and had no time to play. By the end of the period all children were expected to attend school and laws regulated where and how long they could work.
Rich Nursery
We played with toys that might have been found in a rich child's nursery.
Foundling Hospital
Henry and I have been reading Hetty Feather, so we had to include the Foundling Hospital. It was admissions day, so our older Foundlings helped bath and bed the babies.
Cotton Mill
We worked in the Cotton Mill, sweeping cotton fluff from under the machines. We timed ourselves to see if we could work fast enough to stay out of the way of the "moving" machines.
Mine
We filled up coal trucks and pushed them through the mine shaft. Those who wanted to rubbed some coal dust on their face to look the part.
Chimney Sweep
Legally children under the age of 10 were already banned from climbing chimneys but we know from records of accidents that younger kids were still being sent up there. We climbed along our less dangerous horizontal chimney.
Street Sellers
We made pies and cakes to sell on the street, along with flowers, fruit and watercress.
School
We attended a Victorian school, writing on slates and being threatened with the cane and dunce's cap.
Toys
We played with a whipping top, cup and ball, and other Victorian toys. We also tried bowling a hoop and stick (it's really hard!).
Making Toys
We made Jack in the Boxes, spinning tops, peg dolls, optical illusion toys and moving paper toys.
