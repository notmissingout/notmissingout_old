Little Learners Explore Victorian Communication
The Victorian period witnessed a communications revolution. The telegraph, pre-paid postage stamps, the travelling post office, and even the telephone were invented during Victoria's reign.
Writing Letters
Most Victorian letters and records would have been written using a quill pen and ink so we had a try with them.
Using a Typewriter
The typewriter was invented during the Victorian period and started to be used by businesses to send letters. We tried typing messages on a typewriter and discovered that there's no way to delete things when they go wrong.
5 Needle Telegraph
We sent messages using a five needle telegraph based on the first UK commericial telegraph system made by Cooke and Wheatstone.
Morse Code Telegraph
We sent messages using a morse code telegraph similar to that which would have been used at the very end of the Victorian period.
Secret Codes
People invented secret codes to use when sending telegraphs (sometimes because the information was secret, sometimes to reduce the length of the message and thereby save money). Some of us tried using codes to write secret messages.
Telegraph Game
The telegraph helped to catch criminals who could no longer jump on a train to escape justice. A description of the offender could be telegraphed up the line and the police arrest them at the station. We played a dice game to see if the criminal could reach the station before the telegraph (they didn't of course).
Delivering the Mail
Some children dressed as letter carriers and delivered letters whilst others delivered parcels.
Sorting the Mail
We sorted letters and parcels in our own travelling post office.
Post Office
We sold stamps and franked letters. We tried the red stamp on the penny black and the black stamp on the penny red.
Perforating Stamps
The first stamps had to be cut by hand. The Victorians then invented perforated stamps. They used a special machine, but we created the same effect by punching holes with a needle.
Wrapping Parcels Before Sticky Tape
We wrapped parcels in brown paper and string and delivered newspapers.
The Telephone
We learnt that the telephone was invented during the Victorian period (though not used by many people). We held a balloon in order to feel our voice vibrate the air. We then used a string telephone.
Victorian Pillar Boxes
We iced pillar box shaped biscuits in the green colour they were originally painted. We also posted letters through a latter Victorian red pillar box.
The Flying Scotsman
The Flying Scotsman threw out a message in a potato (so that the signalman could telegraph up the line without the train needing to stop) so we practised our potato throwing skills.
The Railway
The railways were important in the story of communication. They were the first customers for the telegraph and carried letters swiftly from one part of the country to another. We used this as an exvuse to play with some wooden mail trains.
Exploring Further
Some of us chose to read books, try themed wordsearches, colouring pictures or Jigsaw Puzzles.
