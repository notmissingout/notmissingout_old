Little Learners Explore Victorian Health
Henry had great fun helping me prepare for this session. He helped make props, prepare signs and of course tried out some role play. We discussed the topic in advance and read some books. Henry was really keen to share what he had learnt with his friends.
Louis Pasteur's Lab
We used a microscope to view bacteria and microscopic organisms. We took swabs to smear on nutrient agar in the hopes of growing a bacterial colony at home, and we helped Pasteur invent a vaccine for chicken cholora.
Invalid Food
We used Mrs Beeton's recipe for oatmeal gruel (measuring in pints and ounces of course) and then those brave enough tasted 3 different types Victorian invalid food.
The Nightingale Home and Training School for Nurses at St Thomas' Hospital
We trained as Victorian nurses. Don't forget to open the windows!
Pharmacy
We sold branded medicines, mixed up remedies, made a health tonic drink, and mixed colours for the shop carboys.
Smog
We made smog in a jar.
Surgery
We pretended to amputate limbs using the techniques available to the early, mid and late Victorians and then draw a card to see whether our patient survived.
Mary Seacole's Hotel
We made food to serve to the troops at the British Hotel, and hunted for rats and Mrs Seacole's pig.
Home Remedies
We 'cured' bruising with vinegar and brown paper.
Sewage and our graveyard
We enjoyed sensory sewage play and discovered why poo was such a health problem for the Victorians. We read some sad facts in the graveyard and looked at genuine Victorian memoriam cards.
Colouring, wordsearches and books
Some of us tried themed wordsearches, colouring, a quiz, or looked at Victorian books.
